# AI-Generated Actor and Deepfake Content Compliance Assessment

Chinese title: AI生成演员与深度伪造内容合规评估：面向合成媒体平台的授权、标识与治理控制

English title: Compliance Assessment for AI-Generated Actors and Deepfake Content: Consent, Labeling, and Governance Controls for Synthetic Media Platforms

## Project Overview

This portfolio project evaluates the compliance risks of a hypothetical synthetic media platform that allows users, creators, brands, or production companies to generate AI-performed short videos using real human likeness, voice, facial features, or motion data.

The project focuses on practical compliance controls rather than a general opinion essay. It treats the author as an AI compliance analyst assessing whether a platform can lawfully and responsibly create, distribute, and commercialize AI-generated actors or deepfake-style content.

## Core Scenario

The platform allows users to upload facial images, video clips, voice samples, motion data, prompts, scripts, and licensing documents. The system can generate AI actors for short drama production, digital advertising, virtual influencers, fan content, and social media videos.

The central compliance question is how the platform can prevent unauthorized use of a real person's face or voice while meeting consent, licensing, labeling, provenance, and incident response obligations.

## Frameworks

This project maps risks and controls against:

- EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations for AI-generated and manipulated content;
- China AI-generated synthetic content labeling requirements;
- SAG-AFTRA AI-related consent, compensation, and digital replica principles;
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework categories: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.

## Four-Week MVP Plan

### Week 1: Project Definition and Risk Baseline

- Define the platform scenario and compliance questions.
- Describe the AI-generated actor system.
- Build the first version of the risk register.

### Week 2: Consent and Licensing Controls

- Develop the consent and licensing checklist.
- Cover authorization scope, compensation, duration, territory, revocation, secondary use, and training-data use.

### Week 3: Labeling and Disclosure Controls

- Draft the AI-generated content labeling policy.
- Compare visible labels, metadata labels, persistent watermarking, and platform-level disclosure.

### Week 4: Framework Mapping and Final Report

- Map controls to EU AI Act Article 50, China labeling rules, SAG-AFTRA principles, and NIST AI RMF.
- Prepare a final 6-8 page compliance assessment report.
