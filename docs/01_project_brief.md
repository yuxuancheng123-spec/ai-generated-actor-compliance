# AI-Generated Actor / Deepfake Compliance Assessment

## 1. Project Background

This project evaluates the compliance risks of an AI-generated actor platform that allows users or production companies to create synthetic video performances based on real human likeness, voice, facial features, or motion data.

The project focuses on synthetic media use cases in short drama production, digital advertising, virtual influencers, and fan-generated content. The core compliance concern is whether AI-generated performances can be created, distributed, or commercialized without violating consent, privacy, publicity, labeling, and platform accountability requirements.

Unlike a general AI ethics essay, this project is designed as a practical compliance assessment. It asks what controls a synthetic media platform should implement before allowing users to generate, publish, or monetize AI actors based on real people.

## 2. Platform Assumption

The hypothetical platform provides AI actor generation services for creators, brands, production companies, and individual users. Users may upload a real person's face, voice, video clip, or motion reference and then use AI models to generate short drama scenes, advertisements, virtual influencer posts, or fan-made content.

The platform may also allow professional actors or performers to license authorized digital replicas of themselves. In this scenario, the compliance challenge is not only preventing unauthorized deepfakes, but also managing lawful digital replica creation through clear consent, compensation, purpose limitation, and content labeling controls.

## 3. System Scenario

The hypothetical platform allows users to:

- Upload facial images, video clips, or voice samples;
- Generate AI-performed short videos;
- Modify the identity, expression, voice, or body movement of a performer;
- Publish or commercialize generated content on social media or short-video platforms;
- Use generated characters in advertising, fan fiction, or short drama production.

## 4. Core Compliance Questions

This assessment asks:

1. What consent and licensing controls are required before creating an AI-generated actor based on a real person?
2. How should the platform distinguish between authorized digital replicas, unauthorized deepfakes, parody, fan content, and fully synthetic characters?
3. What labeling and disclosure obligations should apply to AI-generated or AI-manipulated video content?
4. What risks arise from using facial, voice, and motion data in model training or content generation?
5. What governance controls should the platform implement across the AI content lifecycle?
6. What incident response process should apply when unauthorized likeness or voice use is reported?

## 5. Frameworks Used

This project will map the platform's risks and controls against:

- EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations for AI-generated and manipulated content;
- China AI-generated synthetic content labeling requirements;
- SAG-AFTRA AI-related consent and compensation principles for digital replicas;
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.

## 6. Intended Outputs

The final portfolio will include:

- System description and data flow diagram;
- Risk register;
- Consent and licensing checklist;
- AI-generated content labeling policy;
- Incident response playbook;
- Governance framework mapping;
- Optional deepfake provenance and watermarking review;
- Optional risk scoring demo;
- Final 6-8 page compliance assessment report.

## 7. Scope and Limitations

This project uses a fictional platform scenario for educational and portfolio purposes. It does not provide legal advice. The goal is to translate emerging AI, synthetic media, and performer-rights obligations into practical compliance controls that could be used by an AI short drama platform, AI actor generation service, virtual endorsement company, or synthetic media production workflow.
