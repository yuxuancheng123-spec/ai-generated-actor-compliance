# AI Content Labeling and Disclosure Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines when and how the synthetic media platform must label AI-generated, AI-edited, or deepfake-style content. It applies to content generated, edited, published, downloaded, licensed, or commercially distributed through the platform.

The policy has four goals:

1. Help viewers understand when media is AI-generated or materially manipulated;
2. Reduce deception, impersonation, and unauthorized digital replica risks;
3. Preserve provenance information when content leaves the platform;
4. Create an auditable labeling record for compliance review and incident response.

This policy is a compliance design artifact for portfolio purposes. It does not provide legal advice.

## 2. Policy Scope

This policy applies to:

- AI-generated short drama clips;
- AI-generated advertisements or endorsements;
- AI-generated or AI-modified actor performances;
- Face-swapped, voice-cloned, lip-synced, or body-motion-generated videos;
- Virtual influencer and synthetic character content;
- Fan-generated content using real-person likeness, voice, or style;
- Downloaded content, exported content, thumbnails, previews, trailers, and repostable media.

This policy covers both platform-generated content and user-uploaded content that is edited, transformed, or materially altered by platform AI tools.

## 3. Labeling Principles

The platform should apply labeling controls based on the following principles:

- Transparency: Viewers should not be misled into believing that a real person appeared in or endorsed content when they did not.
- Persistence: Labels should remain attached when content is downloaded, shared, copied, or reposted where technically feasible.
- Layering: High-risk synthetic media should use both visible labels and machine-readable provenance signals.
- Context sensitivity: Commercial, political, sexual, public figure, child, medical, financial, or defamatory contexts require stronger disclosure.
- Least ambiguity: Labels should use clear terms such as "AI-generated," "AI-modified," "synthetic actor," or "digital replica" rather than vague terms such as "enhanced."
- Auditability: Each label should be linked to the generation record, consent record, user account, model version, and publication event.

## 4. Label Categories

| Label Category | Meaning | Example Use |
|---|---|---|
| AI-generated | The core media output was generated by an AI system rather than captured as a real performance | Text-to-video short drama scene with synthetic actors |
| AI-modified | A real image, video, or audio file was materially altered by AI | Face replacement, voice conversion, lip-syncing, background or body editing |
| Digital replica | The content uses a licensed or purported replica of a real person's face, voice, body, or performance | Actor licenses their face and voice for one campaign |
| Synthetic voice | A voice is cloned, generated, converted, or substantially AI-modified | AI-generated dialogue using a licensed voice model |
| Simulated endorsement | A real or synthetic person appears to promote, recommend, or endorse a product, service, political position, or brand | AI actor in an advertisement |
| Unverified likeness claim | The content may resemble or imitate a real person but authorization is not fully verified | Content held for review or blocked from publication |

## 5. Required Labeling Controls

| Content Scenario | Visible Label | Machine-Readable Label | Watermark or Provenance | Human Review | Default Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fully synthetic fictional character | Required if published externally | Required | Recommended | Low-risk review | Allow |
| AI-generated actor based on a licensed performer | Required | Required | Required | Required | Allow within license scope |
| Face swap involving a real person | Required | Required | Required | Required | Hold until authorization is verified |
| Voice cloning or AI voice conversion | Required in audio/video description or UI | Required | Required where supported | Required | Hold until authorization is verified |
| AI-generated advertisement or endorsement | Prominent label required | Required | Required | Enhanced review | Allow only with commercial authorization |
| Public figure, celebrity, politician, or influencer depiction | Prominent label required | Required | Required | Enhanced review | Hold or reject without explicit authorization |
| Child or minor depiction | Prominent label required if allowed | Required | Required | Enhanced review | Reject sensitive uses |
| Political, medical, financial, sexual, defamatory, or sensitive portrayal | Prominent label required if allowed | Required | Required | Enhanced review | Usually reject or escalate |
| User downloads or exports generated content | Label must remain visible where feasible | Required | Required | Automated check | Allow only if label retention passes |
| Reposted or reuploaded synthetic media | Apply platform label if detected | Required for newly hosted copy | Recommended | Risk-based review | Label, restrict, or remove |

## 6. Visible Label Requirements

Visible labels should be clear, close to the content, and difficult to miss. The platform should apply visible labeling in the following places:

- On the video player;
- In the post caption or content description;
- On thumbnails or preview cards when the content is AI-generated or materially AI-modified;
- In advertisement disclosures;
- In user profile pages for virtual influencers or synthetic actor accounts;
- In download pages and export confirmations;
- In content detail pages shown to moderators, customers, or rights holders.

Recommended visible label text:

- "AI-generated video";
- "AI-modified video";
- "Synthetic actor";
- "Licensed digital replica";
- "AI-generated voice";
- "This video includes AI-generated or AI-modified likeness/voice."

For high-risk content, the label should be prominent rather than hidden behind a menu, hover state, or small icon. High-risk content includes public figure depictions, commercial endorsements, political content, child-related content, and any content using a real person's face or voice.

## 7. Machine-Readable Label Requirements

Machine-readable labels should be attached to generated content so downstream systems can detect synthetic media even when visible context is lost.

The platform should include:

- Metadata indicating AI generation or AI modification;
- Generation timestamp;
- Platform identifier;
- Content ID;
- Model or tool category;
- Label type, such as AI-generated, AI-modified, digital replica, or synthetic voice;
- Consent record reference where a real person's likeness or voice is used;
- Hash or provenance record for the generated output;
- Export event record showing whether labels were preserved.

Machine-readable labels should not expose sensitive personal data. If a consent record is referenced, the public file should use an internal reference or token rather than revealing the performer's identity, contract terms, or private documents.

## 8. Watermarking and Provenance Controls

The platform should use layered provenance controls rather than relying on one method.

| Control | Strengths | Limitations | Recommended Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visible watermark | Easy for viewers to notice; survives screenshots if placed well | Can be cropped, blurred, covered, or removed | Required for high-risk or downloadable videos |
| Metadata label | Useful for platforms, search, moderation, and automated review | Can be stripped by file conversion or reposting | Required for all generated or AI-modified exports |
| C2PA or content credentials | Provides structured provenance and tamper-evident records | Adoption varies across tools and platforms | Recommended for professional, commercial, and licensed outputs |
| Platform UI label | Clear inside the original platform experience | Does not travel with downloaded media | Required for all published synthetic media |
| Model-side watermark | Can help later detection if robust | Detection may be uncertain, model-specific, or vulnerable to transformation | Recommended where technically available |
| Hash registry | Supports takedown, repeat-upload detection, and audit | Does not itself inform viewers | Required for high-risk and reported content |

The platform should not rely only on visible watermarks or only on metadata. A layered approach is necessary because each method can fail when content is cropped, compressed, reuploaded, screen-recorded, or edited.

## 9. Label Retention on Download, Export, and Reposting

When users download, export, or share generated content, the platform should:

1. Preserve visible labels for content that uses real-person likeness, voice, digital replicas, commercial endorsements, public figures, or sensitive contexts;
2. Embed machine-readable metadata where the file format supports it;
3. Attach content credentials or provenance records for professional and commercial exports;
4. Warn users that removing labels or misrepresenting synthetic media may violate platform policy and applicable law;
5. Log export events, including user account, content ID, label type, timestamp, and file format;
6. Block export if required labels cannot be attached to high-risk content.

If content is reuploaded without its original label, the platform should use detection tools, hash matching, watermark detection, and user reports to relabel, restrict, or remove the content.

## 10. User Disclosure Obligations

Users must disclose when uploaded or published content:

- Is AI-generated or materially AI-modified;
- Uses another person's face, voice, name, body, or performance style;
- Uses a digital replica, even if authorized;
- Creates the impression of a real person's participation, statement, performance, or endorsement;
- Is intended for advertising, political persuasion, fundraising, medical claims, financial claims, or other high-impact contexts.

The platform should require users to complete a disclosure form before publishing high-risk content. False disclosure should be treated as a policy violation and may result in takedown, account restriction, loss of monetization, or referral to legal review.

## 11. Exceptions and Edge Cases

Limited exceptions may apply, but they should be interpreted narrowly:

- Minor AI edits that do not materially change identity, meaning, or viewer understanding may use lighter labeling;
- Internal testing content may use internal labels only if it is not published, downloaded, or shown to external users;
- Satire, parody, criticism, documentary, or educational uses may still require labeling if a real person is depicted or imitated;
- Fully synthetic characters do not require consent from a real person, but still require AI labeling when published as generated media;
- Newsworthy or public-interest content should receive legal and editorial review rather than automatic exemption.

No exception should allow users to create non-consensual sexual content, defamatory impersonation, fraudulent endorsement, child exploitation, or deceptive political manipulation.

## 12. Moderator Review Workflow

Before publication, the platform should classify the content:

1. Is the content AI-generated, AI-modified, both, or neither?
2. Does it depict, imitate, or resemble a real person?
3. Does it use face, voice, name, motion, or performance data?
4. Is there a consent or licensing record?
5. Is the context commercial, political, sexual, child-related, medical, financial, or defamatory?
6. Which visible label is required?
7. Which machine-readable label is required?
8. Is watermarking or content credentialing required?
9. Should publication, download, monetization, or external sharing be restricted?
10. Is enhanced human review required?

The review decision should be logged with the content ID, label category, reviewer ID or automated system ID, policy rationale, and timestamp.

## 13. User Appeal and Correction Process

Users may appeal a labeling decision if they believe content was incorrectly labeled, over-labeled, or under-labeled. Appeals should be reviewed by a moderator who did not make the original decision where feasible.

The platform should allow:

- Correction from "AI-generated" to "AI-modified" where appropriate;
- Addition of a stronger label if the original label was incomplete;
- Removal of a label only when the content is verified not to be AI-generated, AI-modified, or synthetic media;
- Rights-holder reports when a person believes their likeness or voice was used without consent;
- Viewer reports when content appears deceptively unlabeled.

Appeal outcomes should be documented and linked to the content audit trail.

## 14. Enforcement

The platform may take the following actions for labeling violations:

- Add or correct the label;
- Restrict visibility or monetization;
- Block download or export;
- Remove content;
- Notify the uploader;
- Notify affected rights holders where appropriate;
- Preserve evidence for incident response;
- Suspend or terminate accounts for repeated or serious violations;
- Update model, upload, or review controls if a labeling gap is found.

Repeated attempts to remove AI labels, obscure watermarks, falsify content origin, or market synthetic media as real should be treated as high-severity violations.

This policy directly mitigates the following risk register items:

- R1: Unauthorized likeness use;
- R2: Unauthorized voice cloning;
