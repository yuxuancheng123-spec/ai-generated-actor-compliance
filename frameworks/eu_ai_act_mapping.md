# EU AI Act Mapping

## 1. Purpose

This mapping connects the synthetic media platform's controls to EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations for AI-generated and manipulated content, including deepfake-style image, audio, and video outputs.

This document is a compliance design artifact for portfolio purposes. It does not provide legal advice.

## 2. Relevant Platform Context

The hypothetical platform allows users to generate AI actors, face-swapped videos, voice-cloned dialogue, synthetic advertisements, virtual influencer posts, and fan videos. These outputs may create or manipulate image, audio, and video content in ways that could mislead viewers into believing that a real person appeared, spoke, performed, or endorsed the content.

## 3. Mapping Table

| EU AI Act Article 50 Theme | Platform Risk | Platform Control | Evidence Artifact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transparency for AI-generated content | Viewers cannot tell that a video was generated by AI | Visible AI label on player, caption, thumbnail, and download page | Labeling policy, content UI screenshot, publication log |
| Machine-readable detection of generated or manipulated content | Downstream systems cannot identify synthetic media after export | Metadata label, content credentials, watermark, content hash registry | Export metadata record, C2PA record, hash registry log |
| Deepfake disclosure | A real person's face or voice is manipulated in a way that appears authentic | Prominent "AI-modified" or "digital replica" disclosure | Moderator decision log, label type, consent record |
| Exception-sensitive disclosure | Creative, satirical, artistic, or fictional contexts still risk deception | Narrow exception review with context-specific labeling | Exception review form, editorial/legal review note |
| User-facing transparency | Users publish synthetic media without understanding disclosure duty | Mandatory pre-publication disclosure form | User disclosure attestation, workflow log |
| Provider/deployer responsibility split | Platform and business customer each assume the other will label content | Contract clause assigning labeling responsibilities | Business customer terms, content delivery checklist |
| Misleading endorsement | Synthetic actor appears to endorse a product or brand | Enhanced review for advertising and endorsements | Commercial authorization record, reviewer approval |
| Auditability | Platform cannot prove when and how a label was applied | Immutable content-label audit trail | Content ID, label version, timestamp, reviewer/system ID |

## 4. Required Platform Controls

The platform should implement:

1. Mandatory AI labels for generated or materially AI-modified content;
2. Prominent labels for deepfake-style content involving real-person likeness, voice, or performance;
3. Machine-readable labeling for downloadable and externally distributed content;
4. Export controls that preserve labels, metadata, and provenance records;
5. User disclosure forms for AI-generated, AI-modified, digital replica, and synthetic voice content;
6. Human review for high-risk contexts, including public figures, endorsements, children, sexual content, political content, medical claims, financial claims, and defamatory portrayal;
7. Audit logs connecting source media, consent records, generated outputs, labels, reviewer decisions, and publication events.

## 5. Gap Analysis

| Gap | Compliance Impact | Recommended Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Labels visible only inside platform UI | Labels may disappear when content is downloaded or reposted | Add metadata, watermarking, and content credentials |
| No distinction between AI-generated and AI-modified | Viewers and reviewers cannot understand the type of manipulation | Use label categories defined in `05_labeling_policy.md` |
| No deepfake-specific escalation | Real-person manipulation may be treated like ordinary generated content | Require enhanced review for face, voice, body, and public figure uses |
| No audit trail | Platform cannot prove compliance or investigate incidents | Link labels to content ID, model/tool, consent record, user, and reviewer |
| Business customers control downstream distribution | Platform labels may not persist in ads or social reposts | Add contractual labeling obligations and export verification |
